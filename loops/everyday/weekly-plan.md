# Weekly planning

> Turn a messy week into a prioritized plan with deep work blocks and a shopping list.

## Goal (the what)

- **Objective:** Plan my week from my calendar and task list.
- **Success criteria:** 5 key tasks prioritized, deep work blocks scheduled around existing meetings, and a shopping list for the meals I want to cook.
- **Constraints:** Do not double-book over existing calendar events. Keep deep work blocks at least 90 minutes.
- **Context:** My calendar, my task backlog, and the meals I want this week.

## Loop (the how)

- **Think:** Read the calendar and tasks, decide what matters most this week.
- **Act:** Rank the top 5 tasks, place deep work blocks in open slots, build the shopping list from the meals.
- **Observe:** Check the plan against the calendar for conflicts and against the task list for gaps.
- **Reflect / Plan:** Move blocks that collide, re-rank if priorities shifted.
- **Stop when:** Top 5 tasks are ranked, deep work blocks are scheduled with no conflicts, and the shopping list is complete.

## Starter prompt

```
Plan my week. Review my calendar and task list. Pick and prioritize the 5 most
important tasks. Schedule deep work blocks of at least 90 minutes in open calendar
slots without double-booking. Build a shopping list for these meals: [meals]. Show
the plan, then check it for calendar conflicts and fix any. Stop when the 5 tasks are
ranked, the deep work blocks are scheduled conflict-free, and the shopping list is done.
```
